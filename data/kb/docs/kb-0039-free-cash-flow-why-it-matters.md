# Why Free Cash Flow Matters

**Summary:** Free cash flow (FCF) shows how much cash a company generates after covering expenses and investments. It is important because it indicates the companyâ€™s ability to grow, pay debts, and reward investors. Tracking FCF helps spot financial health and potential risks early.

## Key ideas
- FCF = Cash from operations minus capital expenditures.
- Strong FCF means more money for debt, dividends, and growth.
- Growing FCF often signals improving business performance.
- Declining FCF might warn of financial troubles.
- FCF can be manipulated; always check financial details carefully.

## Simple example
If a company makes $500,000 from operations and spends $200,000 on equipment, its free cash flow is $300,000 ($500,000 - $200,000). This $300,000 can be used to pay debts, distribute dividends, or invest in new projects.

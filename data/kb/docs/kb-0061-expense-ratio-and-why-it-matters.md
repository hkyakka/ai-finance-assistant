# Understanding Expense Ratio and Its Importance

**Summary:** The expense ratio shows the yearly cost to manage and operate a fund, expressed as a percentage of assets. It directly reduces the returns investors receive, making it vital to consider when choosing investment funds.

## Key ideas
- Expense ratio includes management fees, administrative, and operational costs.
- A higher expense ratio lowers overall investment returns over time.
- Comparing expense ratios helps find cost-efficient funds with better net returns.
- Passive funds typically have lower expense ratios than actively managed ones.
- Expense ratio is calculated by dividing total fund costs by the fundâ€™s assets.
- Investors should balance expense ratio with fund performance and strategy.

## Simple example
If you invest $10,000 in a fund with an expense ratio of 1%, you pay $100 annually in fees. If another fund has a 0.2% ratio, your cost drops to $20, making more money stay invested and grow.
